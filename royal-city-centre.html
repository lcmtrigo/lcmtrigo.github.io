Collaborating on a website redesign proposal at Royal City Centre

UX/UI Design, Information Architecture

Completion date: January 2017
Tools used: Sketch, Adobe Creatve Suite, Google Slides

Royal City Centre was the client for my information architecture term project at BCIT. Two other students and I compiled a document proposing a redesign of an existing website based on its current layout. We delivered a formal presentation to our class, summarizing our document and showing our key findings.

As the group’s sales manager, I ensured that we were well rehearsed for the presentation, organizing our slideshow and speaking points. I also overseen the user experience portion of the design brief, defining a target audience, composing user scenarios and designing wireframes.

Problem

Dead links, dead links everywhere


Our group found the following issues with Royal City Centre's website during out first visit:

  • Labelling and navigation were incosistent
  • Store directory page was poorly organized
  • Dead and redundant use of links
  • Repetitive content

Royal City Centre's home page at the time of our first visit

Competitive Analysis

Looking at competitors' websites helped with finding trending design patterns


Our group did a competitive analysis between Royal City Centre and two other mall websites. We made a side by side comparison chart of the websites, using a points system that rewarded whole points if they had certain features and half points if there were any inconsistencies.

Royal City Centre scored the lowest in our comparison was the only website with inconsistencies.

User Experience

Using empathy aided in the discover of user needs


I visited Royal City Centre the weekend after my group’s presentation and found my target audience description to be accurate. There were a lot of seniors at the food court and the working professionals commonly used the parking lot. I also noticed the rise in condos and small apartments in the surrounding area, which I predict will lead to a rise in working professionals.

Site Navigation

The main goal was to remove redundant content


Our group came up with a new site map for Royal City Centre’s website. We took some of the features from competing shopping mall websites as well as reorganized the current navigation.

The purpose of removing redundant content and reorganizing the site map was for a more consistent flow across the site. Adding beneficial features came afterwards.

Proposed site map for Royal City Centre

Wireframes

Make it look good but not too good


My biggest takeaway from this part of the project was learning how to properly design wireframes for websites and applications. I often felt tempted to take away from the wireframes’ unrefined and low fidelity nature by add things such as color, images and typography. I learned how to use simple graphics styles to represent common website features such as crossed boxes for images and rectangles with drop shadows for call-to-action buttons.

I also learned that content at this stage of the design is used for context. I reflected this in my wireframes by adding written content in areas of the site that needed context and placeholder text in areas that didn’t.

Pricing

Pay half now and the other half later


My role within the group involved researching and negotiating with my peers, competitive pricing for our web design service. I researcbed the average price for a website redesign charged by design agencies and freelancers.

I came up with the idea for a unique financing-eqsue style payment plan, which lets the client pay half the price up front and the other half when the service is finished.

Pricing plan for the design proposal

Reflection

Information architecture is a relationship between users, content and context


The presentation of this document my group made to the class was well recieved. If I could change anything, it would probably be elaborating more on the connection between information architecture and how it plays more of a role in the strategy portion of the design process. When it comes to UX, the fluency in both the language of design and the language of business is important and something I hope improve on next time.
