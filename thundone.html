Partaking in a UI design challenge at Thundone

UI Design, Branding

Time frame: March 2017 to April 2017
Tools used: Sketch, Adobe Creatve Suite

The UI challenge was the term project for my UI design class at BCIT. The goal was to design a sign-up screen, a to-do list and a fitness tracker while maintaining visual consistency across all three interfaces. The interfaces are all within one application and are supposed to “match” in look and feel.

My biggest takeaway from this project was purely focusing on the look and feel of the interface while adhering to a predetermined UX and Information Architecture. It was a good learning experience for me in terms of creating a visual brand identity for a digital product. Sketch was my software of choice because of its simplicity and flexibility in terms of export options.

Planning

Finding inspiration

I got the idea to use a predominantly yellow colour scheme from the taxi interface. I had never designed a yellow UI before and wanted to step out of my comfort zone

The to-do list was one of the main inspirations behind my UI. Clean and subtle lines, good use of typography and carefully thought out information architecture.

My idea for the bar graph came from the running interface. I liked the use of gradients and the use of tabs for week, month and year.

The three interfaces that served as inspiration for my design.

I took the best bits from all the interfaces in the research phase and combined them in my design, starting out with hand drawn sketches.

Interface sketches

High fidelity

A little design feedback led to big improvements

I used Sketch to design the interface for the beta version of Thundone and Photoshop to edit the images. I utilized Google’s material design concept by adding drop shadows on some of the UI elements, giving them that ‘floaty’ paper feel.

Thundone beta version

I submitted the beta version of the UI to an online forum for critique, making changes to the interface after receiving significant feedback. Gone are the two background images for the to-do list and fitness page, leaving only the image for the sign-up page (now in full colour). I also made changes to the icons as well as made a logo for the app.

Thundone refined version

Reflection

Don't let the tools get in the way

At the time I had started Thundone, I was already comfortable with using Sketch for UI design and was the only one in my cohort who used the software for the UI challenge. That doesn't mean I wouldn't have been capable of doing it with Illustrator (althrough the final product may have looked different if I did). Tools come and go but core design principles will stay with you and can be applied to almost any medium.
